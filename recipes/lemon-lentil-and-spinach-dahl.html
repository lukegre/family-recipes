Lemon Lentil & Spinach Dahl

Reheat: stovetop with a splash of water. Excellent for lunch leftovers.

Batch25 minThermomix-friendly

Similar highly rated recipe

Ingredients

  • 200 g red lentils
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp curry spices
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 2 handfuls spinach
  • 1 lemon

Method

  1. Finely chop the onion and garlic. Soften them in olive oil or ghee for 5-6 minutes, then add curry powder, cumin, turmeric or garam masala and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Rinse the red lentils, then add them with coconut milk and enough low-salt stock or water to cover generously. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring often, until the lentils collapse into a creamy dahl.
  3. Stir through spinach at the end so it wilts but stays green. Finish with lemon juice, taste the adult portion for salt, and loosen with hot water if leftovers thicken.
  4. Serve with rice, yoghurt and extra lemon. For Thermomix, chop onion and garlic first, saute with spices, then cook lentils with liquid before stirring in spinach.

Why it nourishes

Lentils bring steady plant protein, iron and fibre; lemon helps the body absorb more of the iron from the spinach and lentils.

For your little one

For 10-month-old Keep spices mild. Mash or blend a portion; serve with rice. Avoid adding salt to the baby portion.

Leftovers / reheating

Reheat: stovetop with a splash of water. Excellent for lunch leftovers.
