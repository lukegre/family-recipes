Lemon Lentil & Spinach Dahl

Batch25 minThermomix-friendly

Ingredients

  • 200 g red lentils
  • 1 onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp curry spices
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 2 handfuls spinach
  • 1 lemon
Method
  • Cook onion, garlic and spices.
  • Add lentils, coconut milk and water or stock.
  • Simmer until soft, then stir in spinach and lemon.

Reheat: stovetop with a splash of water. Excellent for lunch leftovers.

For 10-month-oldKeep spices mild. Mash or blend a portion; serve with rice. Avoid adding salt to the baby portion.

Chicken, Sweet Potato & Broccoli Traybake

Batch40 minHands-off

Ingredients

  • 600 g chicken thighs
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes
  • 1 head broccoli
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp tahini + lemon sauce
Method
  • Roast chicken and sweet potato first.
  • Add broccoli later so it does not burn.
  • Finish with tahini-lemon dressing.

Reheat: covered in oven at 170°C. Good Monday anchor meal.

For 10-month-oldOffer soft shredded chicken, mashed sweet potato, and very soft broccoli florets. Cut into safe finger-food sizes.

Chickpea Tomato Feta Skillet

Recovery20 minLow effort

Ingredients

  • 2 cans chickpeas
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 150 g feta
  • 1 handful herbs
Method
  • Simmer tomatoes, garlic and chickpeas.
  • Add feta at the end.
  • Serve with rice, potatoes or GF bread.

A strong Thursday meal: warm, fast, and forgiving.

For 10-month-oldSquash chickpeas flat to reduce choking risk. Keep feta minimal because it is salty.

Green Power Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 courgettes
  • 250 g frozen peas
  • 2 handfuls spinach
  • 1 can white beans
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

  • Cook vegetables and beans with stock.

Baked Eggs & Greens

Recovery25 minProtein-rich

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs
  • 500 ml tomato base
  • 2 handfuls spinach
  • 1 handful herbs
  • 100 g optional feta
Method
  • Warm tomato base in pan.
  • Add greens.
  • Crack in eggs and bake or cover until set.

Best fresh, but sauce base can be made ahead.

For 10-month-oldCook eggs fully until firm. Chop spinach finely. Serve with soft toast fingers or potato.

Thai Curry

Turkey or Lentil Bolognese

Batch35 minFreezer-friendly

Ingredients

  • 500 g turkey mince or 250 g red lentils
  • 700 ml passata or canned tomatoes
  • 1 carrot, 1 celery stick, 1 onion
  • 1 tbsp Italian herbs
  • 400 g GF pasta or potatoes
Method
  • Cook the veg base slowly if you can.
  • Add mince or lentils and tomatoes.
  • Simmer until rich.

Reheat: stovetop with splash water. Make double and freeze sauce.

For 10-month-oldKeep baby portion low salt. Serve sauce with small pasta, potato, or soft veg; mince should be finely broken up.

Golden Chicken, Rice & Greens Pot

Batch35 minOne-pot

Ingredients

  • 600 g chicken thighs
  • 300 g brown rice or basmati
  • 3 carrots
  • 2 handfuls spinach or kale
  • 1 tsp turmeric, ginger and garlic
  • 200 g Greek yoghurt
Method
  • Soften garlic, ginger and carrots with turmeric.
  • Add rice, chicken and stock; simmer until rice is tender.
  • Shred chicken, stir in greens, and serve with yoghurt.

A gentle all-in-one meal for tired weeks: protein, carbs, greens and enough softness for leftovers.

For 10-month-oldKeep stock low salt. Shred chicken finely, mash carrots, and chop greens small.

Beef, Lentil & Hidden Veg Ragu

Batch40 minIron-rich

Ingredients

  • 500 g beef mince
  • 150 g red lentils
  • 700 ml passata
  • 1 carrot, 1 courgette, 1 onion
  • 1 tbsp Italian herbs
  • 400 g GF pasta or polenta
Method
  • Cook onion, grated carrot and courgette until soft.
  • Add beef, lentils, passata and herbs.
  • Simmer until rich; loosen with water if needed.

Make a double batch. It freezes well and brings iron, zinc and fibre without feeling worthy.

For 10-month-oldBreak mince up finely and keep salt out. Serve with small pasta, potato, polenta or soft veg.

Thermomix Carrot, Ginger & Red Lentil Soup

Batch25 minThermomix

Ingredients

  • 600 g carrots
  • 200 g red lentils
  • 2 cm ginger
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 700 ml stock
  • 1 lime or lemon
Method
  • Chop carrot and ginger, then saute briefly.
  • Add lentils, stock and coconut milk; cook until soft.
  • Blend smooth and finish with citrus.

Good as dinner with eggs, toast or cheese; excellent as a lunch thermos the next day.

For 10-month-oldKeep ginger gentle and stock low salt. Serve thick on pre-loaded spoons with yoghurt to cool.

Sardine Tomato Pasta

Recovery15 minOmega-3

Ingredients

  • 2 tins sardines
  • 500 ml passata or 300 g cherry tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 handfuls spinach
  • 400 g GF pasta
  • 1 lemon
Method
  • Cook pasta while garlic and tomatoes simmer.
  • Fold in sardines and spinach.
  • Finish with lemon and olive oil.

Fast, mineral-rich and surprisingly satisfying. Use tuna or salmon if sardines are a hard sell.

For 10-month-oldCheck carefully for bones and choose low-salt fish if possible. Flake finely and cut pasta small.

Warm Salmon, Pea & Potato Salad

Flexible25 minFresh

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 800 g baby potatoes
  • 250 g frozen peas
  • 1 cucumber or 2 handfuls greens
  • 200 g Greek yoghurt
  • 1 handful dill and 1 lemon
Method
  • Boil potatoes, adding peas near the end.
  • Pan-cook or bake salmon.
  • Toss with yoghurt, dill, lemon and greens.

Works warm for dinner and cold for lunch, which is useful when reheating is awkward.

For 10-month-oldCheck salmon for bones, flake well, and squash peas. Serve cucumber only if soft or finely chopped.

Baby-Friendly Chicken a la King

Batch30 minBaby-friendly

Ingredients

  • 500 g chicken thighs or breast
  • 2 carrots
  • 200 g mushrooms
  • 150 g frozen peas
  • 300 ml whole milk
  • 200 ml low-salt chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp flour or cornflour
  • 300 g rice, potatoes or toast
Method
  • Cook chicken gently, then remove and shred.
  • Soften carrots and mushrooms, then make a mild creamy sauce with milk, stock and flour.
  • Stir in peas and shredded chicken; serve over rice, potatoes or toast.

A mild creamy chicken and veg dinner that works over rice, potatoes or toast fingers.

For 10-month-oldKeep the sauce unsalted and skip stock cubes unless low-salt. Shred chicken finely, chop mushrooms small, squash peas, and serve with soft rice or potato.

Fallback7 minNo-cook

Ingredients

  • 300 g hummus
  • 4 boiled eggs or 400 g cooked chicken

  • 250 g cherry tomatoes
  • 1 pack GF crackers or toast

Method

For evenings when cooking would tip the whole day over. Still balanced enough to count.

For 10-month-oldSpread hummus thinly, quarter tomatoes lengthwise, and cut egg or chicken into safe soft pieces.
