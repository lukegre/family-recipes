Lemon Lentil & Spinach Dahl

Batch25 minThermomix-friendly

Ingredients

  • Red lentils
  • Onion, garlic
  • Curry spices
  • Coconut milk
  • Spinach
  • Lemon
Method
  • Cook onion, garlic and spices.
  • Add lentils, coconut milk and water or stock.
  • Simmer until soft, then stir in spinach and lemon.

Reheat: stovetop with a splash of water. Excellent for lunch leftovers.

For 10-month-oldKeep spices mild. Mash or blend a portion; serve with rice. Avoid adding salt to the baby portion.

Chicken, Sweet Potato & Broccoli Traybake

Batch40 minHands-off

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs
  • Sweet potato
  • Broccoli
  • Olive oil
  • Tahini + lemon sauce
Method
  • Roast chicken and sweet potato first.
  • Add broccoli later so it does not burn.
  • Finish with tahini-lemon dressing.

Reheat: covered in oven at 170°C. Good Monday anchor meal.

For 10-month-oldOffer soft shredded chicken, mashed sweet potato, and very soft broccoli florets. Cut into safe finger-food sizes.

Quinoa Loaded Bowl

Batch25 minModular

Ingredients

  • Quinoa
  • Roasted vegetables
  • Chickpeas or feta
  • Greens
  • Olive oil or yoghurt sauce
Method
  • Cook quinoa.
  • Roast vegetables.
  • Serve with protein and sauce.

Tip: keep sauce separate; eat warm or room temperature.

For 10-month-oldServe deconstructed: soft quinoa, mashed chickpeas, roasted veg strips. Go easy on salty feta.

Chickpea Tomato Feta Skillet

Recovery20 minLow effort

Ingredients

  • Chickpeas
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Feta
  • Herbs
Method
  • Simmer tomatoes, garlic and chickpeas.
  • Add feta at the end.
  • Serve with rice, potatoes or GF bread.

A strong Thursday meal: warm, fast, and forgiving.

For 10-month-oldSquash chickpeas flat to reduce choking risk. Keep feta minimal because it is salty.

Green Power Soup

Recovery20 minThermomix

Ingredients

  • Courgette
  • Peas
  • Spinach
  • White beans
  • Olive oil
Method
  • Cook vegetables and beans with stock.
  • Blend smooth.
  • Top with olive oil, seeds or cheese.

Reheat: Thermomix or stovetop. Add bread, eggs or cheese for extra protein.

For 10-month-oldServe thick in a small bowl or on pre-loaded spoons. Add yoghurt to cool it quickly.

Baked Eggs & Greens

Recovery25 minProtein-rich

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Tomato base
  • Spinach
  • Herbs
  • Optional feta
Method
  • Warm tomato base in pan.
  • Add greens.
  • Crack in eggs and bake or cover until set.

Best fresh, but sauce base can be made ahead.

For 10-month-oldCook eggs fully until firm. Chop spinach finely. Serve with soft toast fingers or potato.

Thai Curry

Flexible25 minFamily favourite

Ingredients

  • Curry paste
  • Coconut milk
  • Vegetables
  • Chicken, tofu or chickpeas
  • Rice
Method
  • Simmer curry paste and coconut milk.
  • Add protein and vegetables.
  • Serve with rice.

Make it saucy so leftovers reheat well.

For 10-month-oldKeep baby portion mild before adding extra curry paste or chilli. Serve rice with soft veg and shredded protein.

Tacos / Wraps

Flexible15 minAssemble

Ingredients

  • GF wraps or corn tortillas
  • Beans, mince or chicken
  • Avocado
  • Tomato / salsa
  • Yoghurt sauce
Method
  • Warm the protein.
  • Put everything on the table.
  • Everyone assembles their own.

Good Saturday meal. Also useful for using up leftovers.

For 10-month-oldServe fillings separately: avocado, beans squashed flat, soft veg, shredded chicken. Avoid hard raw pieces.

Saucy Gnocchi

Flexible30 minComfort

Ingredients

  • Gnocchi
  • Tomato or pesto sauce
  • Spinach
  • Optional sausage, beans or feta
Method
  • Cook gnocchi.
  • Heat sauce with greens and protein.
  • Combine generously.

Key rule: keep it saucy; dry gnocchi is poor without a microwave.

For 10-month-oldCut gnocchi into small pieces. Keep sauce mild and not too salty.

10-minute Egg Fried Rice

Fallback10 minBad-day meal

Ingredients

  • Cooked rice
  • Eggs
  • Frozen peas / vegetables
  • Tamari
  • Sesame oil if available
Method
  • Scramble eggs.
  • Add rice and vegetables.
  • Season with tamari.

Keep frozen veg and eggs stocked at all times.

For 10-month-oldUse low-salt tamari or leave seasoning out of baby portion. Ensure peas/veg are soft; egg fully cooked.

Soup + Eggs + Bread

Fallback10–15 minNo thinking

Ingredients

  • Frozen soup or jarred soup
  • Boiled or fried eggs
  • GF bread / potatoes
  • Olive oil
Method
  • Warm soup on stove.
  • Add eggs and bread or potatoes.
  • Finish with olive oil.

A realistic fatigue meal. Not glamorous, but solid.

For 10-month-oldUse low-salt soup if possible. Serve thick soup with fully cooked egg and soft bread fingers.

Yoghurt Bowl Dinner

Fallback5 minEmergency

Ingredients

  • Greek yoghurt
  • Nuts or seeds
  • Berries / banana
  • Nut butter
  • Honey if needed
Method
  • Assemble in bowls.
  • Add extra nuts/seeds for staying power.

Best as an emergency adult meal, not the main family default.

For 10-month-oldUse plain full-fat yoghurt. Avoid whole nuts; use smooth nut butter or ground seeds.

Turkey or Lentil Bolognese

Batch35 minFreezer-friendly

Ingredients

  • Turkey mince or red lentils
  • Passata / canned tomatoes
  • Carrot, celery, onion
  • Italian herbs
  • GF pasta or potatoes
Method
  • Cook the veg base slowly if you can.
  • Add mince or lentils and tomatoes.
  • Simmer until rich.

Reheat: stovetop with splash water. Make double and freeze sauce.

For 10-month-oldKeep baby portion low salt. Serve sauce with small pasta, potato, or soft veg; mince should be finely broken up.

Moroccan Chickpea & Carrot Stew

Batch30 minPlant-based

Ingredients

  • Chickpeas
  • Carrots
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Cumin, cinnamon, paprika
  • Rice or quinoa
Method
  • Cook spices briefly in oil.
  • Add carrots, tomatoes and chickpeas.
  • Simmer until soft and sweet.

Reheat: stovetop. Add yoghurt or feta for protein and richness.

For 10-month-oldKeep spices gentle. Squash chickpeas and make carrots very soft.

Salmon Potato Traybake

Batch35 minOmega-3

Ingredients

  • Salmon fillets
  • Baby potatoes
  • Green beans or broccoli
  • Lemon
  • Olive oil
Method
  • Roast potatoes first.
  • Add salmon and greens for the last 12–15 min.
  • Finish with lemon and olive oil.

Best fresh. Leftovers work cold/room temp as a lunch bowl.

For 10-month-oldCheck carefully for bones. Flake salmon; serve with mashed potato and soft green beans.

Red Pesto Beans on Toast

Recovery12 minPantry meal

Ingredients

  • White beans
  • Red pesto
  • Spinach
  • GF toast
  • Parmesan or feta
Method
  • Warm beans with pesto and splash water.
  • Wilt in spinach.
  • Serve on toast with cheese.

Very good Thursday option: minimal chopping, high satisfaction.

For 10-month-oldUse pesto sparingly because it can be salty. Squash beans and serve on soft toast strips.

Method

Coconut Noodle Soup

Recovery20 minComforting

Ingredients

  • Rice noodles
  • Coconut milk
  • Stock
  • Frozen veg
  • Tofu, chicken or prawns
Method
  • Simmer coconut milk and stock.
  • Add protein and veg.
  • Add rice noodles at the end.

Make only what you need; noodles are less good as leftovers.

For 10-month-oldCut noodles short. Keep broth low salt and temperature safe; offer soft veg/protein pieces.

Baked Potato Bar

Flexible10 + 45 minLow effort

Ingredients

  • Large potatoes
  • Beans or tuna
  • Cheese
  • Yoghurt / sour cream
  • Salad bits
Method
  • Bake potatoes until soft.
  • Put toppings on the table.
  • Everyone builds their own.

Hands-off and child-friendly. Good weekend option.

For 10-month-oldServe mashed potato with yoghurt, soft beans, or cheese in small amounts. Avoid tough potato skin.

Frittata with Potatoes & Greens

Flexible30 minLeftover-friendly

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Cooked potatoes
  • Spinach or courgette
  • Cheese
  • Herbs
Method
  • Warm potatoes and veg in pan.
  • Add beaten eggs and cheese.
  • Finish under grill or in oven.

Good warm or cold. Useful for lunches without reheating.

For 10-month-oldCook egg fully. Cut into soft strips or cubes; chop greens finely.

Fallback8 minNo-cook

Ingredients

  • Tinned tuna
  • White beans
  • Tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon
Method
  • Mix everything.
  • Serve with bread, potatoes or rice cakes.

High protein, high convenience. Not toddler-first, but excellent adult fallback.

For 10-month-oldUse tuna occasionally and choose low-salt if possible. Squash beans and cut tomato small.

Ingredients

  • Frozen soup
  • White beans or lentils
  • Olive oil
  • Seeds / cheese
  • GF bread
Method
  • Reheat soup on stove.
  • Add beans or lentils.
  • Top with oil, seeds or cheese.

Turns a light soup into a proper dinner.

For 10-month-oldKeep baby portion low salt. Blend beans/lentils into soup or mash well.
